# Early-Access Cohort Selection Criteria

Internal reference for `beta-recruiter`. ICP tier is the primary filter; these criteria are the secondary filters applied when the Tier A pool is deeper than the founder wants to invite in a single round.

---

## Hard excludes

Never invite users who:

- Are Tier C or Tier D per `waitlist-tracker.md`
- Work at a direct competitor (check the Company column against `competitive-landscape.md` if available)
- Have given an invalid or disposable email at signup (mailinator, guerrillamail, etc.)
- Have explicitly opted out of outreach in their waitlist note
- Are already in `beta-user-tracker.md`

## Secondary ranking signals

When Tier A is deeper than the target cohort size, rank by:

### Engagement signal (strongest)

- Replied to a Project 04 content piece (email, LinkedIn, Reddit)
- Left a substantive note at waitlist signup (two sentences or more, not just "interested")
- Came from a high-intent source (founder LinkedIn, industry report download) vs. low-intent (drive-by homepage)

### Recency

- Signed up in the last 30 days (warm intent) over 6+ months ago (may have solved the problem elsewhere already)
- Recent activity with the brand (opened the last newsletter, viewed the product page in the last 14 days) — only if this data is available

### Use-case specificity

- Waitlist note describes a specific pain, workflow, or job-to-be-done
- Role and company context make the use-case predictable without a discovery call

### Diversity across the cohort

- First cohort benefits from a spread of company sizes and use-cases, not five identical personas
- Avoid accidental clustering (e.g., five users from the same company or five SDRs at Series A SaaS)
- One or two "adjacent" users from Tier B can be valuable if they represent a use-case that might expand the ICP

## De-prioritize (Tier A-minus)

These are Tier A users who should be invited to a later wave, not the first cohort:

- Signed up but never engaged with any content afterwards
- Left a short generic note ("cool product, interested")
- Work at a company much larger or smaller than the ICP band (edge case for the tier)
- Role-matched but unclear buying authority at their company

The first cohort is your highest-leverage feedback group. Later waves can absorb Tier A-minus.

## Cohort size guidance

- **First cohort:** 8–15 users. Small enough to give each one individual attention, large enough to see patterns.
- **Second cohort:** expand to 15–30 once you have processed the first round of synthesis.
- **Third cohort and beyond:** scales with founder capacity for check-ins and synthesis, not with waitlist size.

Founders who invite 40+ users in the first cohort almost always end up ignoring the long tail of the cohort. Fewer, better-served users produce better learning.

## When to pause recruitment

Pause recruitment and do not draft new invitations when:

- The existing cohort has too many users in `invited` status with no acceptance (>50% unaccepted after 14 days suggests the invitation copy or the target list is wrong)
- The existing activated users have not generated any feedback yet (adding more users before the feedback motion works will make synthesis harder, not easier)
- There is no `product-overview.md` yet, or it has changed substantially since the last recruitment round (the invitation would mis-describe the product)

Surface the pause reason to the founder explicitly rather than silently continuing.
