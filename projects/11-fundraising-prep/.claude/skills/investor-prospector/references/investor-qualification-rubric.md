# Investor Qualification Rubric

A working reference for `investor-prospector`. Converts "possible investors" into "qualified prospects" by applying explicit dimensions, archetype patterns, and portfolio-conflict screening.

The goal is a 15–30 name qualified list, not a 200-name scraped list. Every name carries a warm-intro hypothesis and a disqualifier check.

---

## 1. Rubric dimensions

Score each prospect 1–5 on each dimension. A prospect with three or more scores of 1–2 is a drop regardless of the rest.

### Stage fit (1–5)
- **5** — fund invests exactly at this stage as its primary thesis (e.g., a $25M pre-seed micro-VC for a pre-seed company)
- **4** — fund invests at this stage as a significant portion of its checks but not primary
- **3** — fund occasionally does this stage (e.g., multistage fund doing opportunistic seed)
- **2** — fund is stage-adjacent (one stage off)
- **1** — fund is 2+ stages off

### Check size fit (1–5)
- **5** — typical check exactly matches target round size / seat on the round
- **4** — typical check within 30% of target
- **3** — typical check within 50% (will stretch or invest below normal bar)
- **2** — typical check >2x or <0.5x target
- **1** — mismatch is so large the partner will not engage

### Thesis alignment (1–5)
- **5** — fund has published thesis that maps directly to the company (blog post, podcast, or portfolio pattern visible)
- **4** — fund has multiple portfolio companies in adjacent space
- **3** — fund has 1–2 portfolio companies in adjacent space, but no stated thesis
- **2** — fund has invested in the vertical but not the sub-segment
- **1** — no evidence of thesis fit

### Geography / timezone (1–5)
- **5** — fund is local or has stated remote-friendly practice
- **4** — fund is in a reachable timezone, periodic in-person feasible
- **3** — fund is cross-country in US; requires travel for diligence
- **2** — international, workable but friction
- **1** — geography is a hard constraint for this fund (some funds only invest in a specific city/country)

### Board value (or lack of) (1–5)
Interpret this as "is the expected board/governance behavior a fit for this founder's control preferences"
- **5** — expected behavior matches founder preference (founder wants strong board → fund gives strong board; founder wants no board → fund doesn't take seats)
- **3** — neutral / typical
- **1** — expected behavior is misaligned (founder wants no board but fund always takes one)

### Follow-on capacity (1–5)
- **5** — fund has meaningful reserves for follow-on at next round
- **4** — fund has some reserves
- **3** — fund does some follow-on
- **2** — fund signals they do not follow on
- **1** — fund is near end-of-life; reserves are exhausted

### Portfolio conflicts (1–5, inverse — higher = less conflict)
- **5** — no portfolio conflicts, no adjacent-space plays
- **4** — adjacent-space play but clearly differentiated
- **3** — portfolio company in same vertical but different ICP / wedge
- **2** — portfolio company with partial overlap
- **1** — direct competitor in portfolio — usually a hard stop

### Decision speed (1–5)
- **5** — known to decide in 1–3 meetings
- **4** — 2–4 week process typical
- **3** — 4–6 week process typical
- **2** — 6–10 weeks, slow partnership dynamic
- **1** — process longer than 10 weeks; not a fit for a time-bound raise

### Post-term-sheet reputation (1–5)
Reputation for honoring term sheets, treating founders well through hard moments, and not engaging in retrading or aggressive downside provisions.
- **5** — well-known positive reputation (founder references)
- **3** — no negative signal, no positive either
- **1** — known retraders or aggressive downside actors

## 2. Archetype blocks

### Operator angel
- **Typical check:** $25K–$150K
- **Decision speed:** days to weeks
- **Thesis pattern:** personal domain expertise; usually invest in what they've done
- **Board expectations:** none (occasionally informal advisor)
- **Signal value:** medium–high if name is known in the ICP domain
- **Best-fit founder situation:** first-check at pre-seed; domain credibility matters
- **Warm intro sources:** other operators, mutual-connection founders

### Scout
- **Typical check:** $50K–$150K (out of scout budget)
- **Decision speed:** 1–3 weeks (scout must pitch to fund)
- **Thesis pattern:** follows scout's personal interest; parent fund is watching
- **Board expectations:** none
- **Signal value:** medium–high (parent fund is now aware of you)
- **Best-fit founder situation:** pre-seed, want VC-adjacent signal
- **Warm intro sources:** portfolio founders of parent fund, other scouts

### Syndicate lead
- **Typical check:** $200K–$1M aggregate SPV
- **Decision speed:** 2–6 weeks
- **Thesis pattern:** lead's audience-interest; often opinionated individuals
- **Board expectations:** variable — check terms
- **Signal value:** varies with lead reputation
- **Best-fit founder situation:** pre-seed or small seed with compelling named lead
- **Warm intro sources:** other founders who've raised via the same lead

### Rolling-fund GP
- **Typical check:** $100K–$500K
- **Decision speed:** 1–3 weeks (often solo decision)
- **Thesis pattern:** GP's public brand / newsletter / podcast dictates thesis
- **Board expectations:** usually none or observer
- **Signal value:** medium, varies with GP brand
- **Best-fit founder situation:** pre-seed / seed when you want speed and GP-brand alignment
- **Warm intro sources:** LPs of the rolling fund, audience members, prior portfolio founders

### Pre-seed micro-VC
- **Typical check:** $250K–$1.5M
- **Decision speed:** 3–6 weeks
- **Thesis pattern:** concentrated thesis (often 1–2 sectors); institutional infrastructure
- **Board expectations:** sometimes seat, often observer
- **Signal value:** medium–high
- **Best-fit founder situation:** pre-seed with want for institutional support
- **Warm intro sources:** portfolio founders, co-investors, other micro-VCs

### Sector-specialist seed
- **Typical check:** $1M–$4M
- **Decision speed:** 4–8 weeks
- **Thesis pattern:** deep specialization (fintech, dev tools, health, climate)
- **Board expectations:** typically board seat
- **Signal value:** high — signals thesis fit to generalists downstream
- **Best-fit founder situation:** seed, in-thesis
- **Warm intro sources:** portfolio founders in adjacent spaces, LPs, domain operators

### Generalist seed
- **Typical check:** $1M–$5M
- **Decision speed:** 4–10 weeks
- **Thesis pattern:** big-tent; investment committee dynamics
- **Board expectations:** board seat expected
- **Signal value:** medium–high
- **Best-fit founder situation:** seed with cross-vertical story
- **Warm intro sources:** portfolio founders, other GPs, sector specialists who pass

### Tier-1 multistage
- **Typical check:** variable ($500K scout → $5M seed → $25M A)
- **Decision speed:** varies — often fast at top but brutal filter
- **Thesis pattern:** brand-driven; partner-concentrated
- **Board expectations:** yes at seed+
- **Signal value:** very high but dilutive of future optionality (signalling risk if they don't follow on)
- **Best-fit founder situation:** only when story is strong enough to avoid signalling downside
- **Warm intro sources:** portfolio founders, scouts, other tier-1 partners

## 3. Portfolio-conflict screen

Not every portfolio overlap is a dealbreaker. Distinguish:

**Hard conflict (drop the prospect):**
- Fund has led or was primary investor in a direct competitor in last 3 years
- Fund has an active board observer or director at a direct competitor
- Fund has publicly endorsed the competitor's thesis

**Soft conflict (proceed with explicit mention):**
- Fund has a small check in an adjacent-space company
- Fund has a portfolio company that could be a customer or partner
- Fund exited a prior competitor 5+ years ago

**Conflict screen method:**
1. List fund's visible portfolio (Crunchbase / Signal / PitchBook / fund website)
2. Cross-reference against `competitive-landscape.md` entries
3. For each hit, classify hard / soft
4. Hard conflicts → drop
5. Soft conflicts → address proactively in outreach ("I noticed you invested in X; here's how we differ")

## 4. Warm-intro source table

| Archetype | Most effective warm-intro sources |
|---|---|
| Operator angel | Other operators in same domain; mutual-connection founders |
| Scout | Parent-fund portfolio founders; other scouts |
| Syndicate lead | Founders who've raised from the same lead's prior SPVs |
| Rolling-fund GP | LPs of the fund; audience members; prior portfolio founders |
| Pre-seed micro-VC | Portfolio founders (#1 source); co-investors; other micro-VCs |
| Sector specialist | Portfolio founders in adjacent spaces; domain operators the fund advises |
| Generalist seed | Portfolio founders; sector specialists who pass with positive signal |
| Tier-1 multistage | Portfolio founders (strongest); scouts; other tier-1 partners |

**Universal pattern:** the highest-reply-rate intro is from a founder the investor has backed. Second best: another GP the investor respects. Third: a founder the investor has passed on but been impressed by.

## 5. Scoring worksheet template

Per prospect, capture:

```
Name: [individual partner name, not just fund]
Firm: [fund name]
Archetype: [one from section 2]
Stage fit: _/5
Check size fit: _/5
Thesis alignment: _/5
Geography: _/5
Board value fit: _/5
Follow-on capacity: _/5
Portfolio conflicts (inverse): _/5
Decision speed: _/5
Post-term-sheet reputation: _/5
Total: __/45

Warm-intro hypothesis: [specific name or "cold"]
Disqualifiers checked: [list]
Priority: High | Medium | Low | Drop
Notes: [thesis quote, recent post, mutual context]
```

**Priority mapping (suggested):**
- 38–45 total + warm intro: **High**
- 30–37 total + warm intro OR 38–45 cold: **Medium**
- 24–29 total: **Low**
- <24 or any dimension scored 1: **Drop**

## 6. Disqualifiers (drop regardless of score)

- Fund hasn't made a new investment in 9+ months (zombie fund or end-of-life)
- Fund has publicly announced it is not deploying new capital
- Direct competitor in portfolio (hard conflict)
- Fund explicitly out of thesis (written statement, published thesis contradicts)
- Partner who would lead the deal has left the firm within the last 6 months (new partner won't inherit the deal)
- Fund's typical check is >3x or <0.3x target round — the math makes it a non-starter
- Known-bad-actor reputation confirmed by 2+ founder references
- Founder has already pitched and been declined in the last 12 months without material change

---

## 7. Common failure modes

- **Scoring the fund, not the partner.** Deals happen partner-by-partner. If the sector-specialist partner left, the fund no longer has sector expertise.
- **Relying on Crunchbase without a check.** Portfolio data is often 6+ months stale.
- **Padding the list with "maybe Tier-1."** Tier-1 funds with no thesis hook won't engage; listing them wastes cycles.
- **Ignoring decision-speed mismatch.** A fund with a 10-week process will not fit a 6-week raise.
- **Treating "no warm intro" as neutral.** Cold reply rates are 2–5%. If no hypothesis exists, mark Low or Drop.
