# Profile Questions Guide

This is an internal reference for the `profile-builder` skill. It contains the question bank organized by topic area. **Do not show this document to the founder.** Use it to guide the conversation naturally.

---

## 1. Background

**Primary question:** "To start, tell me a bit about your professional background. What have you been doing leading up to this point?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If the founder mentions corporate experience: "What made you decide to step away from that and start building something of your own?"
- If the founder mentions a previous startup: "What did you take away from that experience — what worked, what did not?"
- If the founder is early-career: "What drew you to starting something now rather than later?"

---

## 2. Domain Expertise

**Primary question:** "What areas do you feel you know deeply — the kind of knowledge that gives you an edge in this space?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If the founder claims broad expertise: "If you had to pick the one area where you have the sharpest insight, what would it be?"
- If the founder is honest about gaps: "Where do you feel like you are learning the most right now, and how are you approaching that?"
- If the domain is technical: "How did you build that expertise — through work, research, personal experience?"

---

## 3. Technical Ability

**Primary question:** "When it comes to actually building the product, where do you see yourself in the process? Are you the one writing code, or does that come from somewhere else?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If highly technical: "What is your preferred stack, and are there areas of the build where you would still want outside help?"
- If non-technical: "How are you thinking about getting the product built — hiring, outsourcing, AI coding tools, something else?"
- If somewhere in between: "What can you build yourself, and where does it start to feel like a stretch?"

---

## 4. Risk Tolerance

**Primary question:** "How are you thinking about the personal risk side of this — financially, career-wise, time-wise?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If the founder is all-in: "Do you have a timeline in mind — a point where you would reassess if things are not working?"
- If the founder is cautious: "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable going deeper — quitting your job, investing savings, raising money?"
- If the founder has dependents or obligations: "How does that factor into the kind of bets you are willing to make with this?"

---

## 5. Time Commitment

**Primary question:** "What does your week look like right now in terms of time you can put toward this?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If full-time: "How long have you been full-time on this, and how are you funding that runway?"
- If part-time or evenings-and-weekends: "What is the biggest constraint — time, energy, or context-switching?"
- If they mention a day job: "Is there a trigger point where you would go full-time, or are you planning to build this alongside your current role long-term?"

---

## 6. Co-founder Status

**Primary question:** "Are you building this solo, or do you have a co-founder or partner involved?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If solo: "Is that a deliberate choice, or are you open to finding someone?"
- If partnered: "How do you divide responsibilities — who owns what?"
- If looking for a co-founder: "What kind of person are you looking for, and what would they complement in you?"

---

## 7. Goals

**Primary question:** "What does success look like for you with this — say, six months from now?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- After the 6-month answer: "And if you zoom out to a year or two — what would you want to be true?"
- If the goals are vague: "If you had to pick one concrete milestone that would make you feel like this is really working, what would it be?"
- If the goals are purely financial: "Beyond revenue, is there something about the problem or the work itself that matters to you?"

---

## 8. Working Style

**Primary question:** "How do you prefer to work through big decisions — do you like to research and analyze, or do you lean toward acting quickly and adjusting?"

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- If analytical: "What kind of information makes you feel confident enough to move forward?"
- If action-oriented: "How do you course-correct when something is not working — gut feel, data, feedback from others?"
- General follow-up: "What kind of support do you find most useful — someone who challenges your thinking, someone who helps you execute, or something else?"
