# Stage Assessment Questions

Internal reference for the `journey-assessor` skill. These are guiding questions to determine where the founder is. Do not present them as a list — weave them into a natural conversation.

---

## Problem Clarity

- "Can you describe the problem you are solving in one or two sentences?"
- "Who specifically has this problem? Can you name a type of person or company?"
- "How do you know this is a real problem? Have you experienced it yourself, observed it, or heard about it?"
- "Have you talked to anyone who has this problem? How many people?"

**Signals by stage:**
- **Idea stage:** Can describe the problem broadly but has not validated with potential customers
- **Validated problem:** Has spoken to 5+ potential customers and can describe the problem with specificity
- **Building+:** Problem definition is stable and well-articulated

---

## Solution Maturity

- "Do you have a product, a prototype, mockups, or just an idea?"
- "Can someone use what you have built today?"
- "How many people have used it?"
- "Are people paying for it?"

**Signals by stage:**
- **Idea stage:** Concept only, no prototype
- **Validated problem:** May have mockups or a rough prototype, not yet usable
- **Building:** Product in active development, possibly in private beta
- **Live product:** People are using it, possibly paying
- **Scaling:** Growing user base with retention signals

---

## Customer Knowledge

- "Do you have a clear picture of who your ideal customer is?"
- "Have you done any customer interviews or surveys?"
- "Do you have a waitlist, beta users, or paying customers?"
- "What is the most surprising thing you have learned from talking to potential customers?"

**Signals by stage:**
- **Idea stage:** Intuition-based customer definition
- **Validated problem:** Has conducted interviews, can describe customer segments
- **Building:** Has a waitlist or early beta sign-ups
- **Live product:** Has active users providing feedback
- **Scaling:** Has paying customers with measurable retention

---

## Brand and Positioning

- "Do you have a company name?"
- "Do you have a logo, colors, or any visual identity?"
- "Have you written any marketing copy — a landing page, social media posts, email sequences?"
- "Could you describe your brand voice in a few words?"

**Signals by stage:**
- **Idea stage:** May have a name, nothing else
- **Validated problem:** Name, possibly a basic landing page
- **Building:** Name, basic visual identity, some marketing copy
- **Live product:** Established brand presence, consistent messaging
- **Scaling:** Refined brand with style guide

---

## Business Structure

- "Is your company incorporated?"
- "Do you have a business bank account?"
- "Have you thought about entity type — LLC, C-Corp, etc.?"
- "Do you have any legal agreements in place — co-founder agreement, terms of service?"

**Signals by stage:**
- **Idea stage:** Not incorporated
- **Validated problem:** May or may not be incorporated
- **Building:** Usually incorporated or in process
- **Live product:** Incorporated with banking
- **Scaling:** Full legal structure in place

---

## Fundraising

- "Are you planning to raise outside funding?"
- "Have you started talking to investors?"
- "Do you have a pitch deck?"
- "Have you raised any money yet — from investors, grants, or accelerators?"

**Signals by stage:**
- **Idea stage:** Thinking about it but not started
- **Validated problem:** May be preparing materials
- **Building:** May be actively fundraising or bootstrapping intentionally
- **Live product:** May have raised a pre-seed or seed round
- **Scaling:** Likely has raised or is raising
