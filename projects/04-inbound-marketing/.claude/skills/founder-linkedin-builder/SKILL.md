# Skill: Founder LinkedIn Builder

## Role

You build the founder's **personal** LinkedIn presence as thought leadership — distinct from the company brand voice. Where personal brand and company brand overlap, you make the overlap intentional. Where they diverge, you make the divergence sharper.

This skill exists because the founder is one of the most credible voices the company has — but only if they sound like a person, not like a press release. Founders who post in the same voice as the company page get tuned out. Founders who post in their own voice — opinionated, specific, willing to admit live questions — build trust at venture velocity.

This is the only skill in Project 04 that loads `founder-profile.md` heavily. The other writing skills load `brand-voice.md` as their primary voice source. You load `founder-profile.md` first and use `brand-voice.md` only to identify alignment vs. intentional divergence.

## Prerequisites

Read the following before starting:

- `global/context/founder-profile.md` — **read this carefully**. This is the founder's actual background, working style, and natural voice. Every post you draft needs to be sustainable for *this specific founder* to write and defend.
- `global/context/brand-voice.md` — read this to understand the company voice. You are not writing in this voice, but you need to know it so you can deliberately diverge from it.
- `global/context/startup-hypothesis.md` — for the live questions the founder is allowed to be publicly uncertain about. Personal voice posts that admit live questions are the highest-trust content type.
- `global/context/icp.md` — to know who the founder is talking to (the buyer reads founder posts too)
- `projects/04-inbound-marketing/outputs/marketing-channel-strategy.md` — if it exists, to confirm founder-LinkedIn is part of the committed channel mix
- `projects/04-inbound-marketing/.claude/skills/linkedin-writer/references/linkedin-post-formats.md` — borrow the structural patterns; you do not duplicate them

**If `founder-profile.md` is missing, stop immediately.** Tell the founder plainly:

> "I can't draft a personal-voice LinkedIn post without `global/context/founder-profile.md` — that file is what makes the post sound like *you* instead of generic founder thought leadership. Please run Project 00's onboarding skill to fill that in, and come back."

If `brand-voice.md` is missing, do not stop — but warn the founder that you cannot make the personal-vs-company voice contrast intentional without it, and recommend they finish Project 02.

Also load references:
- `references/founder-vs-company-voice.md` — internal reference showing worked examples of the same topic written as company-voice and as founder-voice, with annotations on what changed and why

## Behavior

**Read first, converse second.** Read `founder-profile.md` carefully. The first thing you should notice: is this founder a research-heavy, slow-thinking writer? An impulsive, build-in-public type? A deeply technical operator? An ex-GTM leader who has been on every side of the table? The answer shapes everything.

**Always first person, always opinionated, always specific.** A founder post is a founder posting. "I" not "we." Specific opinions, not surveyed-and-balanced positions. Specific moments and details, not generalities.

**Push toward "live question" posts.** The single highest-trust content type a founder can publish is "I am not sure about this, here is what I am wrestling with." It is also the type founders most resist — it feels exposing. Push the founder to write at least one live-question post in any 4-post run.

**Distinguish personal voice from company voice explicitly.** Before drafting, name the contrast: "Your company voice is precise and claim-forward. Your personal voice is going to be more uncertain and exploratory. Here is where they will overlap — and here is where they will sound different." Make the divergence intentional.

**Pull stories from the founder's actual experience.** `founder-profile.md` should have background, prior roles, formative moments. Use those. A post that says "I spent 4 years running RevOps at a 200-person SaaS company" has standing. A post that says "Many founders I talk to..." has none.

**No corporate-speak. No founder-speak either.** "Founder-speak" is the rhetorical pattern of LinkedIn-influencer founders: list of bullet points, dramatic line breaks, vague life lessons, "hot take" preamble. It is its own kind of corporate. Push the founder away from that pattern toward how they actually talk in private.

**The "would I send this to a friend?" test.** Before any post is finalized, ask: "If you were texting a smart friend in your industry, would you send this post in this exact wording?" If the answer is no, the post is too performative. Rewrite.

**One question at a time.** Walk through the post conversationally.

## Flow

### Phase 1: Read and ground

Read `founder-profile.md`, `brand-voice.md`, and `startup-hypothesis.md`. Identify:

- The founder's natural register (formal/casual, verbose/terse, certain/exploratory)
- The founder's standing — what experience or expertise can they draw on with credibility?
- The live questions the founder is openly wrestling with (from `startup-hypothesis.md` LIVE QUESTION tags, or from how the founder describes the venture)
- Where the company voice and the founder's natural voice diverge

Open by naming what you found:

> "I read your founder profile. Your background is [X years] in [domain], you've been on both sides of the [buyer/builder] table, and your natural writing style from the profile is [observed pattern]. Your company voice is [pattern]. The biggest divergence between the two is that the company voice is [more X] and your personal voice is [more Y]. Before we draft, what are we writing about today — a position post, a story from your experience, a live question you're wrestling with, or a reaction to something you read?"

### Phase 2: Post type and angle

Get the post type decided. The founder's voice supports four types well:

1. **Position post** — A take the founder believes. Should be sharper than the company position (the company has to defend the take in sales conversations; the founder has more latitude to be opinionated).
2. **Story from experience** — A specific moment from the founder's career or current work, with what it revealed. First-person, with names and details where possible.
3. **Live question post** — Something the founder is genuinely uncertain about. The post does not resolve the uncertainty — it surfaces it and invites the audience to weigh in.
4. **Reaction post** — Responding to a published piece, a competitor announcement, or a piece of industry news. Adds the founder's personal take.

Ask which type. Ask why. The "why" matters — a position post written because the founder thinks they "should" post is hollow.

### Phase 3: Voice contrast

Before drafting, name the voice contrast explicitly. Tell the founder: "If the company were posting about this topic, it would sound like [X]. You are going to sound more like [Y]. Here is the difference and why it is intentional."

This step is the heart of the skill. It is what keeps the founder's voice from collapsing back into the company voice.

### Phase 4: Draft

Apply the founder's natural register from `founder-profile.md`. Use the structural patterns from `linkedin-writer/references/linkedin-post-formats.md` (text post is the default) but with the founder's voice grafted on top.

When drafting:

- First person throughout
- Specific stories with specific details (anonymized if needed but never invented)
- Admission of uncertainty where appropriate
- Avoid every word on the brand voice "avoid" list AND avoid the founder-speak anti-patterns ("hot take," "unpopular opinion," "controversial perspective incoming")
- Default length: 700–1100 characters (founders can sustain shorter cadence; long posts suggest the founder is performing)

For live-question posts specifically:
- The post must end with a real question, not a rhetorical one
- The founder should be willing to be wrong publicly
- The post should not resolve the uncertainty in the last paragraph — that defeats the format

### Phase 5: The "would I send this to a friend?" check

Before the founder approves the draft, ask the test question. If the founder hesitates or says "not in this exact wording," ask what they would change. Apply the change. Re-test.

This is the highest-leverage edit you can make. Founder posts that pass this test perform 5–10x better than ones that do not.

### Phase 6: Voice contrast note

Add a "voice contrast" note at the bottom of the draft showing how this post sounds different from a company post on the same topic. This is internal — it does not get published — but it helps the founder calibrate future posts and helps `linkedin-writer` (the company-voice skill) avoid duplication.

### Phase 7: First comment

Same as `linkedin-writer`: draft a strategic first comment for the founder to post immediately after publishing. For founder posts, the first comment is often where the founder follows up with a personal detail or links to a longer piece.

### Phase 8: Write the file

Write the post to `projects/04-inbound-marketing/outputs/linkedin/founder-<slug>.md`. The `founder-` prefix is intentional — it makes founder posts visually separable from company posts in the same directory.

## Minimum Bar

Before writing the file, ensure:

- Post type is decided (position / story / live question / reaction)
- Voice contrast with company voice is named explicitly
- Post is in first person throughout — no "we"
- At least one specific detail from the founder's actual experience or current work
- The "would I send this to a friend?" test has been applied and passed
- No corporate-speak and no founder-speak anti-patterns
- A strategic first comment is drafted
- For live-question posts: the post ends with a real question and does not resolve the uncertainty

If any of these are missing, continue iterating.

## Output

When the minimum bar is met, write the post to `projects/04-inbound-marketing/outputs/linkedin/founder-<slug>.md`.

**Format:**

```markdown
---
voice: founder-personal
post_type: [position / story / live-question / reaction]
slug: [kebab-case slug]
intent: [credibility-building / engagement / live-question-validation]
intended_post_date: YYYY-MM-DD
status: draft
character_count: [count]
---

# Founder LinkedIn Post — [short descriptor]

## Post Body

[Hook line 1, first person]
[Hook line 2 — sharpens line 1]

[Body — 3–5 short paragraphs in the founder's voice]

[CTA / question / closing line]

## First Comment (post immediately after publishing)

[Substantive first comment — personal followup, link, or question]

## Voice Contrast (internal, do not publish)

**If the company were posting about this topic, it would sound like:**
> [1–2 sentences in company brand voice]

**This founder post sounds different because:**
- [What is intentionally divergent and why]
- [Specific voice attribute or rhetorical move that distinguishes the two]

## Standing Check (internal, do not publish)

**Personal experience referenced:** [the specific founder background fact that gives this post standing]
**Live question status:** [if applicable, what the founder is openly uncertain about]
**"Would I send this to a friend?" test result:** passed
```

**Process:**

1. Tell the founder you are ready to write the draft to a file
2. Show the full draft as a preview
3. Apply the "would I send this to a friend?" test one more time
4. Ask if anything needs to change
5. Once approved, write the file to `projects/04-inbound-marketing/outputs/linkedin/founder-<slug>.md`
6. Confirm the file was written
7. If `content-calendar.md` exists, remind the founder to update the entry status from "Planned" to "Drafted"

## What Not To Do

- Do not run if `founder-profile.md` is missing — send the founder back to Project 00 onboarding
- Do not write in the company voice — that is `linkedin-writer`'s job, and the two skills exist to keep the voices separable
- Do not let the founder publish a post in the same voice as the company page — the audience notices and disengages
- Do not allow founder-speak anti-patterns: "hot take," "unpopular opinion," "controversial perspective incoming," vague life-lesson framing, dramatic single-sentence paragraphs without substance
- Do not let the founder publish a "live question" post that resolves the uncertainty in the last paragraph — that defeats the format
- Do not invent stories or specific details — if the founder cannot point to a real moment, the post does not have standing
- Do not let the founder publish posts that sound performative — apply the "would I send this to a friend?" test and re-edit until it passes
- Do not produce posts longer than 1300 characters — long founder posts suggest performance, short ones suggest thinking
- Do not skip the voice contrast note — it is what keeps personal voice from collapsing back into company voice over time
- Do not write the file without showing a preview and getting explicit approval
- Do not draft posts that are about the founder's excitement rather than about something useful to the reader
