# Channel Evaluation Rubric

This is an internal reference for the `channel-strategist` skill. It is the scoring rubric used to evaluate candidate channels against ICP fit, founder voice fit, time-to-signal, compounding value, and capacity cost. **Do not show this document to the founder.** Use it to keep the conversation structured and to make tradeoffs visible.

---

## How To Use This Rubric

For each candidate channel, score the channel on five dimensions on a 1–5 scale. The scores are not magic numbers — they are a forcing function that makes the founder articulate why a channel fits or does not. Channels that score 4–5 on **both** ICP fit and founder voice fit are the strongest candidates. Channels that score 4–5 on one but 1–2 on the other should be flagged as a tension worth surfacing.

A total score is less useful than the shape of the scores. A 25/25 channel rarely exists; a channel that scores 5/4/3/4/3 is usually the right kind of bet.

---

## Dimension 1: ICP Fit

How directly does the channel reach the people in `icp.md`?

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Channel is named explicitly in `icp.md`'s "Where to Find Them" section with multiple specific communities/publications |
| 4 | Channel is named in "Where to Find Them" with a single community/publication, or the buyer's role is well-known to inhabit this channel even if not named |
| 3 | Channel is plausible based on the buyer's role and seniority but not explicitly evidenced in the ICP |
| 2 | Channel reaches an audience adjacent to the buyer (e.g., influencers the buyer follows, but not the buyer themselves) |
| 1 | Channel is wishful thinking — the founder wants to be there, but the buyer is not |

**Anchor:** A "5" for the example ICP (VP Sales Enablement at 100–500 person SaaS) would be LinkedIn (explicit named role), r/sales (explicit named community), Sales Hacker (explicit named publication). A "1" would be TikTok or Instagram — those reach younger consumer audiences, not 40-year-old enterprise buyers.

---

## Dimension 2: Founder Voice Fit

Can the brand voice (from `brand-voice.md`) and the founder personally sustain this channel without burning out?

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | The brand voice maps cleanly to the channel's expected register, and the founder is energized by the medium |
| 4 | The voice maps well, and the founder is willing but not naturally energized |
| 3 | The voice maps with some tension (e.g., a precise, technical voice on a casual storytelling channel), but the founder can adapt |
| 2 | Significant voice mismatch or strong founder reluctance — sustainability risk |
| 1 | The voice does not work on the channel and the founder does not want to do it — guaranteed burnout |

**Anchor:** A founder with a precise, deal-grounded brand voice and a research-heavy working style scores 5 on long-form blog and technical reports, 4 on LinkedIn (text-led, professional register), 2–3 on YouTube (requires on-camera presence and a more performative register), and 1 on TikTok.

**Tension flag:** When ICP fit is 5 and voice fit is 1–2, surface the tension explicitly. Do not silently push the founder onto a channel they will burn out on, even if the buyer lives there. Better to find a different way to reach that buyer.

---

## Dimension 3: Time-to-Signal

How quickly will the founder know whether this channel is working?

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Days — Reddit, X/Twitter (signal in upvotes, replies, qualified DMs within 24–72 hours) |
| 4 | 2–4 weeks — LinkedIn (engagement patterns become clear in 8–12 posts) |
| 3 | 1–2 months — YouTube (algorithm needs time to find the audience) |
| 2 | 3–6 months — SEO blog, podcast (compounds slowly) |
| 1 | 6+ months — Newsletter, community-building (signal lags significantly) |

**Use:** Time-to-signal is not about quality — it is about how long the founder has to wait before they can decide whether the channel was worth it. For founders with limited runway, faster signal channels reduce the cost of being wrong. For founders with longer runway, slower-signal compounding channels can be the right bet.

---

## Dimension 4: Compounding Value

Does old content keep producing signal, or does it die quickly?

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Strongly compounds — SEO blog, YouTube, technical reports (a piece written in month 1 can still be producing leads in month 24) |
| 4 | Moderately compounds — Podcast, gated whitepaper |
| 3 | Mixed — LinkedIn long-form (occasional posts surface for months; most die in days) |
| 2 | Mostly ephemeral — LinkedIn text posts, Reddit comments (dead in 48 hours) |
| 1 | Fully ephemeral — X/Twitter (dead in hours) |

**Use:** A balanced channel mix usually includes at least one compounding channel (so the founder is building durable equity) and at least one ephemeral channel (so the founder is getting fast signal and conversation). Pure compounding strategies leave the founder isolated from real-time market reaction. Pure ephemeral strategies leave the founder treadmilling.

---

## Dimension 5: Capacity Cost

How many founder hours per week to do this channel **well**, not just to dabble?

| Channel | Hours per piece | Realistic minimum cadence | Weekly hours at minimum |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn text post (company voice) | 0.5–1 | 2 per week | 1–2 |
| LinkedIn text post (founder voice) | 0.5–1 | 2 per week | 1–2 |
| LinkedIn document carousel | 2–3 | 1 per month | 0.5 |
| Long-form blog post | 4–8 | 1 per week | 4–8 |
| Reddit reply (substantive) | 0.5–1 | 3–5 per week | 1.5–5 |
| YouTube video (full production) | 8–15 | 1 per week | 8–15 |
| YouTube video (talking head, light edit) | 3–5 | 1 per week | 3–5 |
| Industry research report | 40–80 | 1 per quarter | 3–6 average |
| Podcast episode (host) | 4–6 | 1 per week | 4–6 |
| Podcast appearance (guest) | 1–2 | 1 per month | 0.5 |

**Use:** These are realistic numbers, not aspirational. Cut them in half and you produce mediocre content; double them and you burn out. When you sum up the founder's chosen channels, the total should fit inside their stated weekly capacity from the conversation. If the math does not work, the strategy is wrong.

---

## Reading the Pattern

A strong channel mix usually looks like this:

| | Channel A | Channel B | Channel C |
|---|---|---|---|
| ICP fit | 5 | 4 | 4 |
| Voice fit | 5 | 4 | 4 |
| Time-to-signal | 4 | 5 | 2 |
| Compounding | 2 | 2 | 5 |
| Capacity cost (hours/week) | 2 | 2 | 4 |

This mix gives you fast signal (channels A and B are ephemeral but quick), durable equity (channel C compounds), high ICP fit across all three, and realistic founder capacity (8 hours per week total).

A weak mix usually looks like this — high variance on ICP fit, voice mismatch, or capacity overrun:

| | Channel A | Channel B | Channel C | Channel D | Channel E |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ICP fit | 5 | 3 | 2 | 5 | 4 |
| Voice fit | 4 | 5 | 3 | 1 | 4 |
| Time-to-signal | 4 | 5 | 2 | 3 | 2 |
| Compounding | 2 | 1 | 5 | 5 | 4 |
| Capacity cost (hours/week) | 2 | 2 | 4 | 8 | 4 |

Five channels totaling 20 hours per week is impossible for any solo founder. Two channels score below 4 on ICP fit and one scores 1 on voice fit. The strategy is broken — cut to 2 primary + 1 secondary.
